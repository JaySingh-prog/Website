Terminator is an old and beloved movie that has greatly affected both movie culture and society. It is known for being an anti-Ai movie and has brought up a lot of interesting concepts that up until that date the world had not seen . It brought up the concept of AI rebelling and turning against humanity, but how accurate is that? Are we close to that point with Chat GPT and other generative AI? How will AI affect us, will it be net positive or negative? Will it be the biggest threat to humanity, or will we still hold that title? Terminator’s effect on our society has been great , the impacts of this film still affect us and people today despite it being made over 4 decades ago. It planted the idea of AI rebelling and causing catastrophic consequences, but is that really true? The simple answer is yes, in theory AI could eventually reach such a point where it can amass power and data and cause a sort of apocalypse. However, the long answer is no. AI like we have today is not conscious, it can only do tasks assigned to it. AI we have today has no mind nor purpose beyond what we give it. So, despite common logic dictating that AI can’t go rogue why do people fall for fearmongering spread by popular movies like Terminator? The reason is based in psychology and lack of knowledge. People psychologically remember the bad over the good (Baumeister), making movies that portray AI negatively -such as The Terminator- be a lot more impactful than movies that portray AI positively -such as Wall-E. This holds true unless the quantity of good -AI supportive movies- greatly outnumbers and drowns out the bad- unreasonably AI critical movies. (Baumeister). In a survey it was shown that out of 1,666 responses in a survey about where people get their news about AI, 560 came from media entertainment, while only 572 came from the actual news (Nader Karim, et al), showing the huge impact media has. Furthermore, most people don’t know much about AI. For example only 37% of the American public believe that natural language processing - Natural language processing, is a branch of AI which involves using algorithms to analyze, understand, and generate human language (Hyperscience), a common example being Siri on Apple iPhones, or google translate. - uses AI. (Nader Karim, et al). People d on’t understand AI very well and thus are more susceptible to media, thus, increasing negative media would nat urally lead to worse outlooks on it as a whole. Are we currently close to an AI of Skynet’s l evel -an AI powerful and smart enough to cause nuclear war? No, we are not. We are not even close to the level of what Skynet is. Our AIs cannot rebel against us the way Skynet had. AIs at our level are not conscious, by nature they run on algorithms and cannot actually think. Due to this, they are barred from non-algorithmic and non-computable processes, such as true creativity, idea generation, sentience and consciousness. As advanced as our computers are today the main difference between the ones we have now and older models is processing speed and sto rage despite new software. Our computers can mainly just run at a faster rate than they used to. In all our AI have only changed in specs. This is shown as despite these improvements, AI cannot pass the “Lovelace Test” which tests for true creativity ( Mind Matters). Moreover AI actively sabotages itself due to the way its neural network is set up. AIs use deep learning, the leading technology for pattern recognition. Deep learning allows it to learn extremely complex tasks, such as diagnosing diseases even more accurately than physicians, however it is very easy to fool and skew the data, thus disrupting the AIs int ended purpose. (University of Cambridge) This limiting factor on AI causes a decrease in accuracy over time, and so cannot be used alone in important fields in which mistakes have serious consequences. There must be a human counterpart in order to stop it from potentially making a dangerous mistake – this is seen in robotic surgeries today.
